# Business Continuity Plan (BCP) – SecurityGroup101

The Business Continuity Plan (BCP) ensures that SecurityGroup101 can maintain essential business operations during and after disruptive events, including cyber incidents, system outages, and physical disruptions.

This plan complements the Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) by focusing on **people, processes, and operational continuity**, rather than only technical system restoration.

---

## Objectives

- Maintain continuity of critical financial services  
- Minimize customer and operational impact during disruptions  
- Enable secure remote and alternate work arrangements  
- Align with **GLBA, PCI DSS, FFIEC**, and enterprise resilience requirements  

---

## Critical Business Functions

- Payment processing and transaction handling  
- Customer account access and support services  
- Financial reporting and regulatory submissions  
- Security monitoring and incident response  

---

## Continuity Strategies

### Remote Work Enablement

- Pre-defined remote work procedures for all critical roles  
- Secure VPN access with enforced multi-factor authentication (MFA)  
- Use of hardened and managed endpoints  
- Clear employee guidance for remote operations during disruptions  

---

### Manual Workarounds

- Temporary manual controls for critical financial operations  
- Documented fallback procedures for key business workflows  
- Defined reconciliation processes following system restoration  

---

### Alternate Operating Model

- Use of cloud-based collaboration and communication platforms  
- Ability to operate in a fully remote environment if primary facilities are unavailable  
- Flexible workforce coordination to maintain essential services  

---

## Dependencies

- Identity and Access Management (IAM) systems  
- Cloud infrastructure and SaaS platforms  
- Communication channels (email, messaging, status pages)  
- Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) and backup systems  

---

## Testing and Training

- Annual BCP exercises aligned with DRP testing scenarios  
- Employee training on remote work procedures and continuity protocols  
- Post-exercise reviews to identify gaps and improve response readiness  

---

## Key Insights

- Workforce readiness is critical to maintaining operations during disruptions  
- Strong identity and access controls enable secure remote operations  
- Manual fallback procedures reduce reliance on system availability  
- Coordination between BCP and DRP is essential for full organizational resilience  

---

## Business Impact

This BCP supports:

- Continuity of revenue-generating financial services  
- Reduced operational downtime during incidents  
- Compliance with regulatory expectations for resilience and availability  
- Improved organizational preparedness for both cyber and physical disruptions  

---

## Summary

The Business Continuity Plan provides a structured approach to maintaining operations during disruptive events.

It ensures that critical services remain available while technical recovery is executed through the DRP, supporting a comprehensive and resilient enterprise GRC program.
