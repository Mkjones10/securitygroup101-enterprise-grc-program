# FFIEC Cybersecurity – Control Alignment

SecurityGroup101 operates within the fintech ecosystem and must align with **FFIEC Cybersecurity Assessment Tool (CAT)** expectations to support partnerships with regulated financial institutions.

This document evaluates current capabilities against FFIEC domains and identifies gaps and remediation strategies.

---

## Scope

This assessment aligns SecurityGroup101 capabilities to core FFIEC domains:

- Governance  
- Risk Management  
- Security Controls  
- Threat Intelligence  
- Security Monitoring  
- Resilience  

---

## FFIEC Domain Alignment

| FFIEC Domain | Required Capability | Current State | Gap Level | Recommendation |
|-------------|--------------------|--------------|----------|----------------|
| **Governance** | Board and executive oversight of cybersecurity | Partial | Medium | Establish formal GRC reporting, define risk appetite, and implement governance cadence |
| **Risk Management** | Enterprise-wide risk assessment and risk program | Partial | High | Conduct annual risk assessments aligned with NIST SP 800-30 and include board-level review |
| **Security Controls** | Access, data protection, and system security | Partial | High | Implement RBAC, enforce MFA, standardize encryption, and apply system hardening baselines |
| **Threat Intelligence** | Monitoring and integrating emerging threats | Minimal | Medium | Integrate threat intelligence feeds and incorporate into SIEM detection use cases |
| **Security Monitoring** | Logging, alerting, and anomaly detection | Partial | Medium | Expand SIEM coverage and improve correlation and detection capabilities |
| **Resilience** | Business continuity and disaster recovery capabilities | Partial | High | Align DRP and BCP with NIST SP 800-34 and conduct regular testing |

---

## Key Gaps Identified

- Lack of formalized governance structure and risk reporting  
- Incomplete enterprise risk assessment program  
- Inconsistent implementation of core security controls (MFA, RBAC, encryption)  
- Limited threat intelligence integration into detection workflows  
- Gaps in monitoring coverage and alerting capabilities  
- Disaster recovery and business continuity processes not fully tested or validated  

---

## Recommendations

- Establish a formal governance model with defined roles, responsibilities, and reporting cadence  
- Implement a recurring enterprise risk assessment process aligned with NIST standards  
- Strengthen identity and access management through MFA enforcement and RBAC  
- Integrate threat intelligence into SIEM and detection engineering processes  
- Expand logging and monitoring across all critical systems and environments  
- Conduct regular DRP and BCP testing to validate resilience capabilities  

---

## Key Insights

- Governance and risk management gaps create downstream weaknesses across all security domains  
- Identity and access control deficiencies increase exposure to unauthorized access  
- Limited monitoring and intelligence integration reduce visibility into active threats  
- Resilience gaps increase operational and financial risk during disruptive events  

---

## Business Impact

This alignment supports:

- Improved readiness for partnerships with regulated financial institutions  
- Alignment with FFIEC cybersecurity expectations and regulatory standards  
- Integration into a unified GRC framework (NIST, ISO 27001, PCI DSS, GLBA)  
- Strengthened enterprise resilience and incident response capabilities  

---

## Summary

This FFIEC alignment identifies critical capability gaps and provides a structured path to:

- Enhance governance and oversight  
- Strengthen risk management practices  
- Improve security controls and monitoring  
- Build resilience through tested recovery strategies  

These findings are integrated into the broader **GRC program, control catalog, risk register, and disaster recovery planning efforts**.
