# Governance Model – SecurityGroup101 GRC Program

This governance model is based on **COBIT 2019** and **ISO 38500** and defines how SecurityGroup101 directs, manages, and monitors information and technology to support secure and compliant fintech operations.

---

## Objectives

- Establish clear accountability for cybersecurity and risk management  
- Align IT and security initiatives with business objectives  
- Enable consistent decision-making and risk oversight  
- Ensure compliance with regulatory and industry frameworks  

---

## Governance Principles

- **Responsibility:** Clear assignment of roles, ownership, and accountability  
- **Strategy:** Alignment of IT and security with business goals  
- **Acquisition:** Risk-informed investment in technology and controls  
- **Performance:** Measurement of IT and security effectiveness through defined metrics  
- **Conformance:** Compliance with laws, regulations, and internal policies  
- **Human Behavior:** Consideration of culture, training, and change management  

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Board of Directors
- Approves risk appetite and strategic security initiatives  
- Receives regular GRC, risk, and compliance reporting  

---

### Executive Leadership (CIO, CISO, CTO)
- Owns enterprise information security and GRC programs  
- Ensures alignment with business strategy and regulatory requirements  

---

### GRC Team
- Conducts risk assessments and compliance activities  
- Manages policy lifecycle and control framework alignment  
- Maintains control catalog and framework crosswalks  

---

### Security Operations
- Monitors threats and security events  
- Responds to incidents and manages security tooling  

---

### IT Operations and Engineering
- Implements and maintains technical controls  
- Ensures systems are securely configured and patched  

---

### Business Owners
- Own risks and controls within their respective domains  
- Participate in risk assessments and remediation efforts  

---

## Governance Artifacts

- Information Security Program Charter  
- Risk Management Policy  
- Access Control, Acceptable Use, and Data Protection Policies  
- Control Catalog and Framework Crosswalks  
- GRC Metrics and Reporting Dashboards  

---

## Decision-Making and Escalation

- Risk decisions are escalated based on impact, likelihood, and regulatory exposure  
- Formal risk acceptance process for residual risk scenarios  
- Regular governance forums (e.g., Security Steering Committee) for oversight and decision-making  

---

## Key Insights

- Clear ownership of risk and controls improves accountability across the organization  
- Executive and board-level involvement strengthens governance effectiveness  
- Integration between GRC, security operations, and IT enables coordinated risk management  
- Defined escalation paths ensure timely and informed decision-making  

---

## Business Impact

This governance model supports:

- Improved visibility into enterprise risk posture  
- Stronger alignment between security, IT, and business objectives  
- Regulatory compliance across GLBA, PCI DSS, FFIEC, and related frameworks  
- Consistent and scalable decision-making across the organization  

---

## Summary

This governance model establishes a structured framework for managing cybersecurity risk and compliance across SecurityGroup101.

It underpins the broader **GRC program, control catalog, risk register, and roadmap**, ensuring accountability, alignment, and effective oversight of enterprise security.
