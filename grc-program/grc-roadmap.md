# GRC Roadmap – SecurityGroup101 (18 Months)

This roadmap outlines a phased implementation of the SecurityGroup101 GRC program, integrating **risk management, compliance, governance, and resilience** into a unified strategy.

---

## Objectives

- Establish a structured and scalable GRC program  
- Align security controls with regulatory and business requirements  
- Improve visibility into enterprise risk posture  
- Enable continuous monitoring and improvement  

---

## Phase 1 – Assessment (0–2 Months)

### Objectives
- Establish a baseline understanding of the current security and compliance posture  
- Identify regulatory obligations and high-risk areas  

### Key Activities
- Conduct enterprise-wide GRC maturity assessment  
- Validate asset inventory and data flows  
- Perform quantitative risk assessment aligned with **NIST SP 800-30**  
- Map applicable regulations and frameworks (GLBA, PCI DSS, FFIEC, SOX, NIST CSF, ISO 27001)  
- Identify high-risk gaps and prioritize quick wins  

---

## Phase 2 – Build (2–7 Months)

### Objectives
- Design governance structures, policies, and control frameworks  

### Key Activities
- Formalize governance model and define roles across board, executives, GRC, IT, and security teams  
- Develop and approve core policies (risk management, access control, data protection, incident response)  
- Build unified control catalog and framework crosswalks  
- Develop DRP and BCP aligned with **NIST SP 800-34**  
- Define GRC metrics and reporting structure (KPIs and KRIs)  

---

## Phase 3 – Implementation (7–14 Months)

### Objectives
- Operationalize controls, monitoring, and compliance processes  

### Key Activities
- Implement or enhance MFA, RBAC, encryption, and centralized logging  
- Expand SIEM coverage and develop detection use cases  
- Establish vulnerability management processes and patching SLAs  
- Implement vendor risk management program  
- Conduct initial DRP and BCP testing and refine recovery procedures  
- Initiate periodic GLBA, PCI DSS, and FFIEC-aligned assessments  

---

## Phase 4 – Monitor and Improve (14–18 Months)

### Objectives
- Establish continuous monitoring, reporting, and improvement processes  

### Key Activities
- Track KPIs and KRIs (e.g., time to remediate, incident frequency, test results)  
- Conduct internal audits and control effectiveness assessments  
- Perform annual risk assessment and framework alignment review  
- Update policies, controls, and roadmap based on lessons learned  
- Provide regular GRC reporting to executive leadership and the board  

---

## Key Insights

- A phased approach enables structured and scalable GRC program development  
- Early focus on risk assessment and governance establishes a strong foundation  
- Integration of monitoring and metrics supports continuous improvement  
- Alignment across multiple frameworks reduces redundancy and improves efficiency  

---

## Business Impact

This roadmap supports:

- Improved enterprise risk visibility and decision-making  
- Alignment with regulatory frameworks (GLBA, PCI DSS, FFIEC, ISO 27001)  
- Increased operational resilience through integrated DRP and BCP  
- Enhanced audit readiness and compliance maturity  

---

## Summary

This GRC roadmap provides a realistic and structured approach for building a mature cybersecurity and compliance program within a fintech environment.

It aligns governance, risk management, control implementation, and resilience into a continuous lifecycle, supporting long-term security and regulatory compliance.
